Landing Site Design

Produced a responsive design for a content writing service’s landing site.

Overview

Client: Altum, Inc. (Startup)

Summary

  • Jenni, Altum’s content writing service, needed a new landing page that reflected Altum’s changing business models and focuses.
  • Worked with the team to reflect the current state of the business and experiment with increasing conversions.
  • Design changes increased conversions and sign ups.

Roles

UX Designer, UI Designer, User Researcher

  • Conducted user tests to test for usability.
  • Created and updated wireframes to more efficiently maintain designs.
  • Worked with Development by handing off wireframes with indicated changes.

Background

Our writing service, Jenni AI, started with information only relating to the core writing service. Overtime Altum saw the need to add and alter content on the landing site to better reflect the Jenni writing service.

Goals

We hypothesized that making both simple iterative changes and major visual overhauls would result in an increase in conversions and signups. Conversions were measured by account creation and signups were measured by entering payment info.

Process

The steps of Altum's design process are:

  • Define. Defining our users and the MVP design.
  • Ideate. Ideating on design possibilities.
  • Test. Testing pre-release ideations.
  • Observe. Observing post-release outcomes.

Define

Our primary target users were SEO marketers and marketing agencies. Their needs included consistently on-time article delivery and SEO-optimized content writing that would rank high on search engines’ organic listings.

Ideate

UI work included:

  • Rapid wireframing in high-fidelity to focus on quick changes that wouldn’t eat up design and development time.
  • Updating UI elements to work better responsively and display the necessary information as efficiently as possible.

The Landing Site required multiple additions and updates to reach our goals.

A testimonial carousel displaying quotes from our satisfied clients.

An AI toolkit page for showcasing our new SaaS approach.

Landing page redesigns that updated the visual design and incorporated additional graphics.

Mobile optimization was included to ensure a properly responsive experience.

A pricing page that goes into more detail about Jenni’s plans without clogging up the main landing page.

Webflow migration that consolidated our landing site elements and let us include more visual appeal.

A blog page both let clients read up on valuable content writing tips and helped jenni.ai rank on search engines.

A site structure flowchart that helped ensure proper navigation between the landing site and the login/signup page.

A login/signup page redesign allowing existing users to login to their accounts and new ones to sign up to make new accounts and/or submit applications (writers and editors).

Page for EasyRead that marketed its capabilities and hosted the tool’s actual interface.

Test

Our main testing methods included:

  • User testing to gain a qualitative perspective on the effectiveness of intended landing site design changes.
  • A/B testing using Crazy Egg, a service that records and highlights user interactions.

Observe

Based on our success metrics of an increase and conversions and an increase in signups, we found that altering the landing site resulted in more conversions and signups for certain changes.

Reflection

I learned that by making simple changes and keeping a good record of wireframes, UI elements, etc. one could provide meaningful impact for the business.
